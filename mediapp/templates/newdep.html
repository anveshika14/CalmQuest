Depression

Answers To All Your Questions About Depression

Depression is hard to describe, especially to those who have never experienced it. It is not just sadness. It is bigger, more debilitating, more engulfing. To make matters worse, it does not physically manifest. This makes it hard for people to empathise or even begin to comprehend.

According to World Health Organization (WHO), 5% people on our planet have depression. Approximately 280 million people are living under the cloud of depression. While treatment exists, a large number of people in the low-income levels do not even receive treatment. Untreated depression could also culminate into death by suicide. Over 700,000 people die by suicide every year. These numbers include men, women, and children. It is not just a personal tragedy but a societal issue as well.

Why do some people get it, and others don’t? Are there any early symptoms that can be nipped in the bud? Anything one can do to prevent it altogether? Is it possible to just snap out of it? There are lot of things one wonders when it comes to depression. Let’s start with the basics.

What is depression?

Depression is a mood disorder characterised by persistent low mood, sadness, and loss of interest in activities that one previously enjoyed. For sadness to qualify as depression these symptoms should be persistent for over 2-3 weeks.

Symptoms

There are lot changes reflected in the behaviour of a depressed person. Some of the common signs to look out for are:

  • Loss of interest in just about anything - There is an innate inability to mindfully engage in just about anything. This can even throw the daily routine for a toss. Things like taking a shower or getting out of bed also become burdensome.
